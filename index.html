02 — Audience & Insight

Every Indian mom has lost the syrup battle.

Urban SEC A/A+ mothers · 28–40 · kids 2–10 · Tier 1 + affluent Tier 2 · reads ingredient lists · pays a premium for trust.

The Insight

The medicine-refusal moment is the most universal, least-talked-about parenting micro-frustration. Brands sell efficacy — but mothers are losing on compliance. We didn't invent the war. We just put it on screen first.

Why She Sees Herself

The chaos is exaggerated. The emotion is real.

We don't shame her for struggling — we show her the struggle is universal.

We don't lecture. We laugh with her, then offer the way out.

"Most kids' wellness products are bought by moms but rejected by kids. That is bad product design." — Wellopia Brand Brief

Big Idea & Craft
04 — The Idea

We reversed the power dynamic.

Parents stop chasing the kid. The kid starts chasing the medicine. Wellopia isn't introduced as a product — it's the moment everything calms down.

Tone — warm, never fearful

The brief explicitly forbids fear-based parenting marketing.

Self-deprecating parent comedy lets mom feel seen, not scared.

Chaos is on the parents. The kid is never afraid. The brand only enters in calm.

Format — vertical, Hinglish, iPhone-shot

9:16 because Reels & Shorts is where the ICP lives.

Hinglish ("Aur?", "kuch aur tareeka hoga na?") — native to Tier 1 moms.

iPhone, lo-fi by design — feels like a real family, not stock-footage commercial.

Three-act story in 35 seconds — Setup, two failed attempts, calm pivot, button. Captions carry the joke for the 85% who watch muted.

Why It Outperforms
05 — Distinctiveness

The category sells fear. We sell relief.

What's Different

  • →
    The kid is the brand advocate — not the doctor, not the mom.
  • →
    The conflict reverses — parents end up hiding it from the kid.
  • →
    Hinglish, sitcom-paced, anti-fear. The category goes serious; we go playful.
  • →
    Compliance is shown, not claimed.

Why It Drives Recall & Preference

  • ★
    Recall: three meme-able assets — the helmet shot, "Aur?", and "Now we hide this one."
  • ★
    Engagement: parents will tag other parents. Shared frustration is the share trigger.
  • ★
    Preference: brands that don't condescend or scare win trust.
